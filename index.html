The Saxophone Family

Different saxes:

  1. Soprano

    The smallest of the four, usually straight (but sometimes curved), with a higher, bright, and piercing sound. It is known for its agility and is often used for melodies in jazz and classical music.
    For more on the soprano, click on the navigation menu above.

  2. Alto

    The 2nd smallest and the most common sax, known for its warm, rich, and flexible tone. It is versatile and popular in jazz, classical, and pop music.
    For more on the Alto, click on the navigation menu above.
